Training Nutrition Routine

Training

Mandatory warm-up

  • Wall Angels — 2 × 10
  • Chin Tucks — 2 × 15
  • Band Pull-Aparts — 3 × 15
  • Doorframe Chest Stretch — 2 × 30 sec

How to run it

  • Hard cap: 20 minutes per session.
  • Move quickly between exercises and keep setup simple.
  • When all sets hit the top of the range with clean form, add a small amount of load next session.
  • If time is tight, finish the full warm-up plus the first 4 exercises before the last accessory.

Nutrition

Meal 1 · Breakfast

Main plate 1 cup (250 g) plain 0% Greek yogurt mixed with 1/2 scoop of whey protein. Top with 1/2 cup blueberries or raspberries. Add 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds or crushed walnuts.

Meal 2 · Morning snack

Option A 2 hard-boiled eggs + 1 part-skim string cheese.
Option B 150 g of 2% cottage cheese topped with sliced cucumbers and black pepper.

Meal 3 · Lunch

Main plate 150 g cooked grilled chicken breast or extra-lean ground beef or turkey. 1 cup cooked quinoa or 1 medium baked sweet potato. 2 cups mixed dark leafy greens, plus 1 tbsp olive oil or 1/4 avocado.

Meal 4 · Afternoon snack

Option A 4 slices, about 100 g, of deli smoked turkey breast wrapped around 1/4 avocado.
Option B 120 g plain Greek yogurt mixed with 10–12 almonds.

Meal 5 · Dinner

Rotation A · Omega-3 support 160 g baked salmon fillet with roasted asparagus or Brussels sprouts tossed in olive oil.
Rotation B · Iron support 150 g lean sirloin steak or bison with sautéed broccoli and onions. Add 1 cup brown rice or wild rice.
Social / alcohol day rule Drop the rice or potatoes, but keep the protein portion and vegetables the same.

Meal 6 · Night snack

Option A 150 g plain cottage cheese dusted with cinnamon.
Option B 1 scoop micellar casein protein powder blended with water and 1 tbsp chia seeds.
Rule Keep this low-sugar and protein-centered.
